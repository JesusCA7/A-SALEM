About A-SALEM

The Story Behind Our Work

A-SALEM began as a personal promise from founder Alice Apendeke to heal trauma, protect the vulnerable, and build peaceful communities across eastern Congo.

Our Story

In 2002, after surviving conflict and violence, Alice Apendeke created A-SALEM to offer support where it was needed most. The name A-SALEM is inspired by the word "Salem," meaning peace. Today, the organization stands for dignity, safety, and restoration for women, children, and families in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

A-SALEM is a grassroots, women-led initiative that partners with local communities to provide healing services, educational pathways, economic training, and emergency relief. Our programs are designed to help survivors grow stronger and find lasting hope.

Our Work

How A-SALEM Makes a Difference

Healing Support

Trauma counseling and support groups restore hope for survivors of violence and displacement.

Educational Access

Scholarships, tutoring, and school supplies help children and young women stay in school.

Income Opportunities

Training in crafts, farming, and micro-business helps families earn income and regain independence.
