About A-SALEM

The Story Behind Our Work

A-SALEM began as a personal promise from founder Alice Apendeke to heal trauma, protect the vulnerable, and build peaceful communities across eastern Congo.

Our Story

In 2002, after surviving conflict and violence in eastern Congo, Alice Apendeke returned to her community with a vision: peace must begin with listening, restoring dignity, and giving survivors a safe place to heal.

The first A-SALEM gatherings took place in small homes and open fields, where women and children shared meals, spoke about loss, and began to find strength in one another. From those early meetings, the name A-SALEM was chosen to reflect a simple truth: true peace is built through care, unity, and action.

Over the years, A-SALEM has grown from a single circle of women into a trusted local movement. Guided by survivors and led by women, the organization supports women and children of both genders with counseling, education funding, economic training, and emergency relief while keeping community voices at the center of every decision.

Our Origin

Alice began A-SALEM after witnessing the deep need for spaces where trauma could be spoken about openly and where women could regain control over their futures. The organization’s first initiative was a small support center offering medical care, counseling, and skills training for women who had been displaced or harmed by violence.

As trust grew, A-SALEM expanded services into education and livelihood programs. Today, the organization still honors its beginnings by focusing on the people most affected by conflict and ensuring they are part of designing the next chapter of peace.

A Journey of Healing

From the first shelter to the current community programs, A-SALEM has grown through the voices and experiences of survivors. Each step forward is rooted in trust, care, and the belief that lasting peace begins with people.

2002

Alice started A-SALEM with a small circle of women meeting to share stories, heal, and support one another.

2005

The first support center opened, offering counseling, medical help, and skills training for displaced women.

2012

Education programs began, helping girls and young mothers return to school and continue their learning.

2024

A-SALEM continues to expand into new communities, supporting peacebuilding, leadership, and resilience.

Our Work

How A-SALEM Makes a Difference

Healing Support

Trauma counseling and support groups restore hope for survivors of violence and displacement.

Educational Access

School fee support, uniforms, and tutoring help boys and girls continue their studies and prevent grade repetition when families cannot afford education costs.

Income Opportunities

Training in crafts, farming, and micro-business helps families earn income and regain independence.
